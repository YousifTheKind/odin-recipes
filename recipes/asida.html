Asida

Description

Asida is a common dish in the Arab world. It is a lump of dough, obtained by stirring wheat flour into boiling water, sometimes with added butter or honey.

Similar in texture to fufu, it is eaten mainly in Middle East and African countries.

Ingredients

  • Sorghum flour (Dura)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Milk
  • Raisins

Steps:

  1. Boil water in a large pot and add a pinch of salt.
  2. Gradually add sorghum flour (Dura) to the boiling water while continuously stirring to avoid lumps.
  3. Keep stirring and cooking the mixture over low heat until it thickens and forms a smooth, dough-like consistency.
  4. Once the mixture is cooked, remove it from the heat and shape it into a smooth, round mound using wet hands.
  5. Serve the Asida on a large platter and make a well in the center to hold a dollop of ghee or butter. It is traditionally eaten by tearing off a piece of the Asida, dipping it in the ghee, and then consuming it.
